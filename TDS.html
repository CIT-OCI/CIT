Technology Development Sites

The Critical Industrial Technologies (CIT) initiative helps new Made-In-Ontario products, hit the market faster.


Technology Development Sites (TDS) are where SMEs can come to develop, test, and showcase their Critical Technology products and services.

TDSs are established by industry incumbents, vendors, and associations in their respective technology and/or sector domains. Each TDS has made significant investments into their next generation infrastructure, platforms, and equipment.

The TDS list is always growing, reflecting the unlimited potential of technology leaders in Ontario.

As part of the CIT program, OCI has partnered with these organizations to extend these capabilities for SMEs to utilize and integrate learnings, assets, and/or new IP into their product/service suites.

There are two ways for Small-and-Medium-Sized-Enterprises (SMEs) to access a Technology Development Site:

Apply to the Technology Access Program for subsidized access to a TDS. Best for: companies that have developed a solution employing Critical Technologies but have not yet had the opportunity to validate in real-life environments for their target market.

Apply to the Development and Commercialization Program for subsidized TDS access plus $50-$200k to derisk and offset the costs of product development activities. Best for: companies with Critical Technology products (fully realized or in-development) that are looking to accelerate commercialization within the core CIT sectors.
